About Better Beds

Truck bed replacement made easier from start to finish.

Since April 2025, Better Beds has helped truck owners find, repair, paint, and install OEM-style pickup beds without bouncing from yard to body shop to installer.

Started in April 2025

A focused truck bed shop that saves customers time.

Better Beds was built around one simple problem: replacing a pickup truck bed is usually way more complicated than it should be. Most customers have to find a bed, pick it up, take it to a body shop, wait on repair and paint, then possibly take it somewhere else for installation.

We bring those steps together in one place. We help find the right bed, repair it when needed, paint it, spray bedliners, schedule the installation, and send the customer home the same day unless they request something different.

What We Know Best

High-demand truck beds, especially Chevy and GMC.

We work with a lot of Chevy Silverado and GMC Sierra beds, and we understand how hard the right rust-free OEM or factory original bed can be to find in good condition.

We sell repaired beds and clean used beds.

Not every bed we sell starts damaged. We do get clean, rust-free OEM and factory original beds in good condition too — they just tend to sell quickly to our regular customers because demand is high.

We know where more beds are.

While searching for inventory, we find many beds that may not fit our current stock needs. That means if you are looking for something specific, it is worth asking us.

Customers travel from all over.

People come from across the country because we specialize in low-supply, high-demand truck beds and can handle the hard parts in one stop.

What Matters Most

Why customers use Better Beds.

Replacing a bed is usually a pain. We bring the hard steps together: find, repair, paint, and install.

Same-day installation

When customers travel to us for a scheduled bed installation, they do not have to leave their truck overnight. The bed has already been repaired, painted, and prepared ahead of time, so installation is completed the same day unless the customer asks for something different. Most customers wait in our air-conditioned lobby for about two hours, then head home.

No disposal fee surprises

We do not charge disposal fees for the old bed. If you want to keep your old bed, we will load it up for you.

Pickup is welcome too

You do not have to let us install the bed. Customers are welcome to pick up a bed and handle installation elsewhere if that works better.

Metal-first repair work

When we repair damage, we work the metal back as close to its original shape as possible so only the minimum necessary body filler is used.

More than beds

We also offer spray-in bedliners and can repair other body damage when needed. If the truck needs body work beyond the bed, ask us about it.

Insurance repair work

We work with insurance companies on truck bed and body repairs. If your damage is covered, we can help with the repair side of the process so you may not have to pay the full repair cost out of pocket. If you are searching for truck repairs that accept insurance, ask us what we can do for your claim.

How-To Guide

How to measure your truck bed.

Measure the inside box length from the inside edge of the box to the inside edge of the tailgate. If you are unsure, send us a photo and we can help.
